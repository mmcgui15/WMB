Cabins

The Plan

Summer passed, and the temperature dropped quickly, just as we were ready to start our foundation work on the cabin. Pouring the concrete slab for the fireplace and doing masonry work below freezing would prevent proper curing, so unfortunately this also meant that the main cabin would have to be put on hold for the winter. After our first 27 degree September night in the tent we realized that if we wanted to survive the winter in New Hampshire, we were going to need to build something small and do it fast. And so we spent about 3 weeks building “The Shed”.
Foundation

We decided it would be best to keep it simple and use 9 boulders as the foundation since the Shed is a temporary home. We spent two days digging, moving, setting, and leveling the 300 - 600 lb rocks. We drilled hole into the granite and cut steel pins to attached the cabin to the boulders.

Logs

We cut down some medium sized pines and milled 6, 16 foot sections to make a grid to hold up the cabin. The boulders do not lie below the frost line, so some settling and shifting is likely to occur, but the logs should distribute the weight well enough that the pins will keep the cabin from shifting considerably.

Frame

A simple timber frame made of true 4x4s with 45 degree bracing.

Walls/Siding

The plan was to make board and batten siding until we put up a half log piece and saw how good it looked. It took about a week of cutting trees, peeling and milling logs to make enough half-log siding, but it was well worth. We sealed the gaps with silicone based chinking, but the siding shrunk as it dried more than the chinking could stretch so it will have to be reapplied. We applied a clear coat to give the cabin a natural looking finish.

Inside

Downstairs in “The Shed” is a small kitchen area, a bed, a small couch, and a wood stove on a stone hearth. The loft is Shelby and Raven's domain, as well as some storage and a 55 gallon drum we fill with water to supply the kitchen sink via gravity. To light the place I made a low power 12V LED circuit connected to some deep cycle batteries. Anytime we need real power we use a generator.
